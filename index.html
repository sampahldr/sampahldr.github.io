Research Interests & Projects

3D Bioprinting and Biofabrication

My research focuses on developing hydrogel-based biofabrication strategies for creating biologically relevant and structurally stable 3D constructs for tissue engineering applications.

Current work includes optimization of GelMA-based bioinks, printability analysis, and long-term structural stability assessment of bioprinted constructs.

Tomographic Volumetric Printing (TVP)

Investigating tomographic volumetric printing techniques for rapid fabrication of complex hydrogel structures with improved dimensional fidelity and preservation capability.

Research includes studying how optical exposure conditions and material compositions influence construct stability over extended culture durations.

Electrospinning and Biomaterial Scaffolds

Developing electrospun microfiber and microtube-based biomaterial scaffolds for musculoskeletal tissue regeneration and biomimetic tissue engineering applications.

The work focuses on fabrication strategies, scaffold morphology, and mechanically enhanced hydrogel systems for biomedical manufacturing.
